Dear lovely glow,

 just out of reach,
you sit so round
and near.

You follow us home
in the back seat,
our foreheads
on your glass.

We wake to another
world, beneath
a bright hole
hard to glance.

But when your shape
weighs high, we watch
you as the grass
turns blue.

You move in months
and us in minutes.
You move behind
some fog.

A solid ghost
folds in its slipper
shell and then
to ink itself.

You come back but
never stay.
